# MAD9034 Deliverables

Every week there will be work for you to complete inside or outside of class. Some exercises will be larger than others.

Summary

  • Warm-up Project (10%)
  • Major Project (40%)
  • Midterm (20%)
  • Final Presentation (30%)

Submission Requirements

All work for the warm-up project and the major project must be completed in Figma.

For group projects, ensure all group members have edit access to the shared Figma file. Additionally, your professors must have edit access to your Figma file for all assignments.

In some cases, you may also need to use a FigJam file alongside your main Figma file. Make sure your professors have access to the FigJam file as well, and submit both the Figma and FigJam files through Brightspace.

# Warm-up Project 10%

In the warm-up project, you'll work in groups to design an Apple Watch fitness app while practicing the User-Centered Design (UCD) process during a 3-week design sprint.


# Part 1: User Research Due: Monday January 13th @6:00pm

Part 1 emphasizes conducting user interviews, a critical step in understanding user needs and ensuring a user-friendly app. This foundational research prepares you for the next phases of the design process.

View Details

# Part 2: Synthesized Research Results Due: Wednesday January 15th @5:00pm

Part 2 focuses on synthesizing your user research into actionable insights. You'll use your Figma file to gather, group, and summarize patterns, culminating in a clear problem statement that will guide the next phase of your project.

View Details

# Part 3: Brainstorming Due: Monday January 20th @6:00pm

Part 3 focuses on brainstorming and creative ideation to develop design concepts addressing your problem statement. Using the Crazy 8s method, you'll generate, refine, and vote on ideas, ultimately selecting and developing the most promising concept for prototyping.

View Details

# Part 4: Prototyping Due: Wednesday January 22nd @5:00pm

Part 4 focuses on prototyping your solution and preparing for usability testing. You'll storyboard key steps in your scenario, assign team roles, and create a complete prototype in Figma, supported by an interview script to guide user feedback effectively.

View Details

# Part 5: Testing Due: Monday January 27th @6:00pm

Part 5 focuses on testing your prototype by observing classmates as they complete assigned tasks. Groups will take turns testing their prototypes and participating as users, gathering detailed feedback to refine their designs for the next phase of the project.

View Details

# Part 6: Share Your Observations Due: Wednesday January 29th @5:00pm

Part 6 involves sharing your team's observations and experiences from the warm-up project through a 2-3 minute oral presentation. Reflect on each phase of the project, highlight key surprises, and ensure everyone participates to showcase your journey and insights effectively.

View Details

# Major Project 40%

You will follow the UX design process based on the project of your choice, to produce weekly deliverables starting Week 05. At the end you will showcase your completed work in a presentation deck.


# Part 1: Product Research Due: Monday February 3rd @6:00pm

Part 1 focuses on conducting product research in groups of 3-4 students. You'll analyze competitors, similar products, and business models while collaboratively documenting insights in a shared Figma project to guide your app design.

View Details

# Part 2: Persona Research Due: Sunday February 9th @11:59pm

Part 2 focuses on conducting persona research to identify one primary and one secondary persona for your product. Working in groups, you'll interview target users, analyze data, and create personas to better understand user behaviors, needs, goals, and challenges, guiding your design process effectively.

View Details

# Part 3: Persona Creation Due: Wednesday February 12th @5:00pm

Part 3 transitions to individual work, where you'll create two detailed personas—a primary user (consumer) and a secondary user (service provider). Using persona research and your insights, you'll develop comprehensive profiles, including demographics, goals, frustrations, and behavioral traits, to guide user-centered design solutions for your project.

View Details

# Part 4: Scenarios Due: Wednesday February 19th @5:00pm

Part 4 focuses on creating a text-based usage scenario for your primary persona. You'll outline how the persona interacts with your app, including user info, goals, tasks, and context, to understand their journey and tie it to their objectives. Complete the scenario in your Figma file.

View Details

# Part 5: Information Architecture Due: Monday March 3rd @6:00pm

Part 5 focuses on creating the information architecture (IA) for your app based on your personas, usage scenario, and competitor research. You'll organize content into groups, create an IA diagram in FigJam, choose a platform, design a navigation structure, and name your app to guide the next stages of development.

View Details

# Part 6: Test Ready Low-Fidelity Prototype Due: Monday March 10th @6:00pm

Part 6 focuses on developing a test-ready, low-fidelity prototype based on your scenario and information architecture. You'll create app screens, test the prototype with classmates, document feedback, refine the design, and integrate annotated screens into your Figma file to validate usability and task flow.

View Details

# Part 7 (Midterm): Mid-Fidelity Wireframes Due: Monday March 19th @5:00pm

Part 7 (Midterm) requires transitioning your low-fidelity prototype into mid-fidelity wireframes using Figma. You'll design six key screens in grayscale, focusing on layout, user flow, and core app functionality, while incorporating feedback from testing to refine usability and interactions.

View Details

# Part 8: Design Critique Due: Wednesday March 19th @5:00pm

Part 8 involves a 2-3 minute presentation where students showcase their midterm mid-fidelity wireframes. This exercise provides an opportunity to receive constructive feedback from peers and refine designs based on their insights before moving onto Visual Design.

# Part 9: Visual Design Due: Wednesday April 1st @7:00pm

Part 9 focuses on applying UX visual design principles to your mobile app using Figma. You'll refine at least six key screens, enhancing usability through thoughtful visual design, while ensuring your Figma file is well-organized with clear frame names and labels.

View Details

# Part 10: Testing Documentation Due: Wednesday April 9th @5:00pm

Part 10 focuses on conducting usability testing for your high-fidelity prototype to validate navigation, task completion, and overall user experience. You'll create a test plan, script, and NDA, conduct tests with 5 participants (2 in-class, 3 outside), document findings, and refine your design based on user feedback, culminating in a usability report for your final project submission.

View Details

# Final Presentation 30%

# Presentation Due: Week 15

The Final Project Presentation is an 8-minute showcase of your mobile application, detailing your design process, testing results, and UX improvements in a clear and professional manner. You'll submit Figma file, ensuring your work tells a cohesive story and aligns with course objectives.

View Details
